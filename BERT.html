The Problem with Reading in a Straight Line

arrow_forward
  • Older models read in a straight line, which creates a fundamental problem.
  • When the model sees the word 'book,' it has to guess the meaning without knowing what comes next.
  • It lacks the 'future' context to understand the sentence correctly on the first pass.

The Breakthrough: A Two-Way Street

sync_alt
  • The breakthrough is a model that is 'deeply bidirectional'—it reads the whole sentence at once.
  • It uses both past ('Let's...') and future ('...a flight') context simultaneously to understand the meaning of a word.
help_center
'So what's the trick? How did the creators of BERT teach a computer to understand context by reading a sentence forwards and backwards at the same time, and why does this new method give us a much better tool for our business?'

The Trick: A Giant Game of 'Fill-in-the-Blanks'

edit_note
  • The creators used a technique called a 'Masked Language Model' (MLM).
  • They didn't ask the model to predict the next word. Instead, they randomly hid words in billions of sentences and trained BERT to guess the missing piece based on the surrounding context.

The Result: An Intuitive Grasp of Language

hub
  • By playing this 'game' billions of times, BERT develops a deep, flexible understanding of language.
  • It learns grammar, nuance, and the complex relationships between words.
  • This creates a single, powerful foundation that can be adapted for many uses.

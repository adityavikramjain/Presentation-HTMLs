The Micro Perspective Pt. 1

Sensation vs. Perception

Sensation

sensorsThe immediate response of our sensory receptors (eyes, ears, nose, mouth) to basic stimuli.

Perception

psychologyThe process by which we select, organize, and interpret these sensations to give them meaning.

color_lens

Sensory Marketing: Vision & Color

Color psychology is a key marketing tool. Colors evoke emotions, attract attention, and can become part of a brand's 'trade dress'.

Sensory Marketing: Other Senses

  • airScent: Connects to memory and emotion via the limbic system.
  • volume_upSound: Influences assumptions through sound symbolism.
  • waving_handTouch: Haptic senses create attachment (the endowment effect).
  • restaurantTaste: A multisensory experience influenced by physics and chemistry.

Stage 1: Exposure

  • hearingAbsolute Threshold: The minimum stimulation that can be detected.
  • compareDifferential Threshold: The ability to detect changes between two stimuli.
  • pinchJust Noticeable Difference (JND): The minimum detectable difference.
filter_center_focus

Stage 2: Attention

Attention is the extent to which processing activity is devoted to a particular stimulus. We are selective about what we attend to, influenced by both personal and stimulus factors.

translate

Stage 3: Interpretation & Semiotics

Interpretation is the meaning we assign to sensory stimuli. Semiotics is the field that studies how signs and symbols create meaning in marketing.

How We Learn: Behavioral Theories

Classical Conditioning

notifications_activeA stimulus that elicits a response is paired with another stimulus that initially does not. Over time, the second stimulus causes a similar response.

Instrumental Conditioning

emoji_eventsThe individual learns to perform behaviors that produce positive outcomes and to avoid those that yield negative outcomes.

school

How We Learn: Cognitive & Observational Theories

Cognitive learning theory views people as problem-solvers who actively use information. Observational learning occurs when we watch the actions of others.

The Role of Memory

  • inputEncoding: Information is placed in memory.
  • inventory_2Storage: Information is retained over time.
  • outputRetrieval: Stored information is found as needed.
  • sentiment_very_satisfiedNostalgia: The marketing power of 'bittersweet' memories.

The Motivation Process

A Need is a basic biological motive; a Want is one way that society has taught us to satisfy it. Motivation is the process of trying to satisfy that need.

Motivational Conflicts

  • looks_twoApproach-Approach: A choice between two desirable alternatives.
  • exposure_plus_minus_1Approach-Avoidance: A choice with both positive and negative consequences.
  • blockAvoidance-Avoidance: A choice between two undesirable alternatives.

Classifying Consumer Needs

  • groupsNeed for Affiliation: To be in the company of other people.
  • boltNeed for Power: To control one's environment.
  • starNeed for Uniqueness: To assert one's individual identity.

Negative Affect

The Self-Concept

The self-concept summarizes the beliefs a person holds about their own attributes and how they evaluate the self on these qualities. Consumption choices are a primary way we express this identity.

The Extended Self

External objects that we consider a part of us comprise the extended self. This can include possessions, places, and even other people.

What is Culture?

Culture is the accumulation of shared meanings, rituals, norms, and traditions among the members of an organization or society. It's the 'lens' through which people view products.

auto_stories

Cultural Stories: Myths

Myths are stories with symbolic elements that represent a culture's ideals. Marketers often use mythic structures (e.g., the hero's journey) in their advertising.

Cultural Ceremonies: Rituals

  • checklistA set of multiple, symbolic behaviors that occurs in a fixed sequence.

A true understanding of consumer behavior is built by adopting a dual perspective: a micro view (the consumer's mind) and a macro view (the consumer in society), with both essential to navigate the modern digital marketplace.

travel_exploreIndiGo is solving the core problem of engine reliability in a rapidly growing, but supply-constrained, aviation market.

The Indian aviation sector is the third largest in the world, fueled by a young, aspirational demographic and significant government investment in infrastructure. However, a global supply chain crisis and fleet-grounding engine issues threaten to destabilize this growth. IndiGo is strategically addressing this with a multi-pronged approach that ensures capacity, diversifies its fleet, and enhances the passenger experience. This deck outlines our path to becoming a global aviation leader by turning this industry-wide challenge into a competitive advantage.

trending_upThe Indian aviation market is poised for explosive growth but remains deeply underpenetrated.

Market Growth

  • leaderboardIndia is the world's third-largest aviation market, with passenger traffic having doubled in the past decade. With a projected growth rate of 6.4% from 2023-2043, India is set to outpace other major economies. The number of domestic passengers is expected to reach 350 million by 2030, up from 155 million in 2024.

Untapped Potential

  • expand_circle_downThis growth is driven by a young, digitally-connected demographic and a rapidly expanding middle class. Despite this, air travel per capita is significantly lower than in the U.S. and U.K., highlighting vast untapped potential. The government's investment of $27 billion in airport infrastructure and its UDAN scheme are accelerating this expansion.

warningA global engine-related supply chain crisis is grounding aircraft, causing a significant and costly capacity bottleneck.

  • precision_manufacturingA significant portion of the global aviation fleet, including up to 75 of IndiGo’s aircraft, is grounded due to a defect in a powdered metal component within Pratt & Whitney engines. This has severely constrained the industry's ability to meet soaring passenger demand.
  • local_shippingThe problem is compounded by a global supply chain bottleneck for spare parts and MRO services, leading to longer repair times and higher costs.
  • monetization_onTo maintain flight capacity, IndiGo is incurring additional costs by acquiring older, less fuel-efficient aircraft on damp-lease arrangements, which negatively impacts operational expenses.

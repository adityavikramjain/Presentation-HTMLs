The Barrier Has Always Been Our Tools' Inability to Genuinely Understand Human Language

Our automation has traditionally relied on rigid, rule-based systems:

IF customer email contains "cancel" OR "unhappy"
THEN

This approach misses all nuance: Are they cancelling because of price? Bad service? A competitor? The system has no idea.

The Core Challenge for Any AI Has Been to Understand One Simple Thing: Context

"I need to get a new mouse for my computer."

"There's a mouse in the kitchen."

Without context, a computer cannot tell the difference. This has been the fundamental barrier.

A 2017 Breakthrough Proposed a Radical Idea: Stop Reading, Start Seeing

"Instead of processing words in a line, what if an AI could look at all of them at the same time?"

A paper titled "Attention Is All You Need" changed everything.

The New Approach: Analyze Every Word in Relation to Every Other Word, All at Once

This creates a "panoramic view" of the entire context.

This "Panoramic View" Is Powered by a Mechanism Called Self-Attention

What is Self-Attention?

A method for the AI to calculate a 'relevance score' between every word in a sentence.

It allows the model to determine which other words are most important to understand the meaning of any given word.

In Practice:

For the word "it", Self-Attention asks:

  • How relevant is "robot"? (Score: 0.1)
  • How relevant is "delivered"? (Score: 0.2)
  • How relevant is "pizza"? (Score: 0.7)

In the Sentence "The Robot Delivered the Pizza," Attention Helps the AI Know "It" Refers to the Pizza

The AI learns to create connections based on these "relevance scores."

Training This New Architecture on Internet-Scale Data Created the Large Language Model (LLM)

Large Language Model

A New Foundation for Artificial Intelligence

At Its Core, an LLM Is an Extremely Powerful Engine for Predicting the Next Word

By training on trillions of words, the model becomes incredibly good at one simple task: given a sequence of text, predict the most probable next word.

Think of it as the most sophisticated auto-complete in the world, one that has mastered context to make its predictions.

Predicting the Next Word on a Global Scale Unlocks Surprising New Abilities

Summarization

LLMs Are Powerful but Not Perfect; They Can "Hallucinate" and Require Human Oversight

warning

Key Considerations

  • Factual Inaccuracy: LLMs are trained to be plausible, not truthful. They can "hallucinate" or make up facts confidently.
  • Data Bias: The model can reflect biases present in its vast training data.
Treat LLMs as a powerful assistant that requires human review and strategic direction, not as an infallible oracle.
